1. Program Status Overview

HPED (Health & Physical Education) is the active program offering dual licensure in Health and Physical Education. The PETE (Physical Education Teacher Education) program is being phased out. No new students are being admitted to PETE.

Advisors are currently working with two student populations:

  • HPED students — all incoming and continuing students in the active program
  • PETE students (phase-out) — remaining students being matriculated through the PETE curriculum to completion

Curriculum Notes

  • KNES 311 — Biomechanics is not required for HPED majors.
  • HPED majors may take any approved math course that fulfills their Core 3 Math requirement.
  • BIOL 220/L — Anatomy and Physiology replaces BIOL 221/L plus BIOL 222/L (8 credits total). If an HPED student has already completed BIOL 221/L, they must still complete either BIOL 222/L or BIOL 220/L (BIOL 220/L is typically the easier single-course option).
  • BIOL 191 — Introduction to Biology for Health Professions has a math prerequisite: MATH 102 (taken concurrently with BIOL 191L), or a grade of C (2.0) or higher in MATH 100, or a score of 30 or higher on ALEKS.
  • Core 8 course: If not already satisfied, guide students to a 3-credit course from: ANTH 212, BIOL 105, CHEM 100, GEOG 101, GEOL 109, PHYS 100, or PHYS 103.
  • DANC 106 — Fundamentals of Dance Movement or KNES 244 — Pedagogy in Creative Movement and Gymnastics Skills and Strategies: While KNES 244 is still offered, HPED students may choose either course.
⚠️ Core 4 Requirement: DANC 106 fulfills the Core 4 (Arts) requirement. If a student takes KNES 244 instead of DANC 106, they must take an additional course to fulfill Core 4.

Professional Phase Gate Courses (Practicum I Courses)

HPED students must have a minimum 3.0 GPA (past two years) to enroll in HPED 302 — Drug Use & Personal Safety Education (HE Practicum I) or KNES 317 — Motor Behavior in Physical Education (PE Practicum I) and to be admitted to the professional phase.

Final Program Year (HPED)

⚠️ Final Program Year: The final year is primarily full-time student teaching.

Methods semester (Fall, Year 4): HPED 402 — Methods in Health Education (6 cr.) and HPED 403 — Methods in Physical Education (6 cr.), with practicum placements 3 days per week.

Capstone semester (Spring, Year 4): HPED 481 — Capstone Internship and Seminar in HPE (12 cr.), with student teaching 5 days per week.

Advise students that outside work and other commitments will be very difficult to manage during this time.

5. Advising PETE Students (Phase-Out)

The PETE program is no longer accepting new students. Remaining PETE students are being advised through to program completion. As PETE courses are phased out, students may need to take HPED courses to fulfill requirements using the substitution list in Section 6.

▶

PETE-Specific Curriculum Notes

  • KNES 311 — Biomechanics is required for PETE majors.
  • Prerequisites for KNES 311: a college-level algebra course (MATH 115, 119, 211, or 273) OR a qualifying placement test score (≥ 20 on BAT or > 60 on ALEKS).
⚠️ Even if a PETE student has already met the Core 3 Math requirement, they must still complete one of the listed math courses (or have a qualifying placement score) to meet the KNES 311 prerequisite.
  • PETE students must complete BIOL 221/L — Human Anatomy & Physiology and BIOL 222/L — Human Anatomy & Physiology II (BIOL 220/L does not apply to PETE).

Professional Phase Gate Course (PETE)

PETE students must have a minimum 3.0 GPA (past two years) to enroll in KNES 324 — Teaching Physical Education in Elementary School and to be admitted to the professional phase.

Building DCPs for PETE Students

As PETE courses are discontinued, advisors should use the substitution list to map remaining PETE students into available HPED courses. When building a DCP:

  1. Conduct a degree audit using the PETE curriculum and note which PETE courses remain available.
  2. Apply substitutions from the tables in Section 6 for any PETE courses that are no longer offered.
  3. Build or update the Degree Completion Plan in SSC.
  4. Meet with the student to review the plan before removing the advising hold.
  5. Report substitutions at end of semester: Contact the Program Coordinator or Corinne so substitutions can be entered into the system (see Section 6).
⚠️ Students who have already started internship classes (KNES 324, KNES 325, KNES 423) should continue in the PETE pathway.
📌 Conditional admission & probation: For guidance on advising PETE students who are conditionally admitted or on probationary status, see Section 7. The same advising steps apply to both HPED and PETE students.

⚠️ Course Exception Request Workflow (All Non-PETE Exceptions)

The following process applies to all course exceptions listed above (transfer credits, HLTH substitutions, practicum exceptions, etc.), excluding PETE ↔ HPED course equivalencies.

  1. Advisor identifies a potential exception while advising the student (including the temporary approved exceptions listed above).
  2. Advisor emails the Program Coordinator (Luciana) and Program Specialist (Corinne) with:
    • Student name and ID
    • Required course (HPED/KNES requirement)
    • Course being used for the exception
  3. Luciana reviews and approves the request.
  4. Corinne submits the approved request for official processing.
  5. Once approved, Corinne forwards the approval email to the advisor and Luciana.
  6. The advisor completes the Course Exception Tracking Form.
  7. The advisor uploads the completed form to SSC.

7. Professional Phase Admission

Both PETE and HPED programs adhere to requirements established by the Teacher Education Executive Board. Consult the Standards for Teacher Education Programs (Towson catalog) and the Maryland Teacher EPP Basic Skills Assessment for detailed information.

Conditional Admission (GPA 2.5–2.99)

Students with a two-year GPA between 2.5 and 2.99 may be conditionally accepted. They have one semester—the conditional semester—to meet one of the following:

  • Option 1: Pass the Praxis I exam during the conditional semester.
  • Option 2: Raise their past-two-years GPA to 3.0 by the end of the conditional semester (that semester’s grades are included in the calculation).
⚠️ Key point: The “first semester in the professional phase” and the “conditional semester” are the same semester. If neither condition is met, the student will not be fully admitted. Students who are not admitted may reapply at any point once they meet the admission requirements.

Students may apply for an exemption, but the COE can approve up to 10% of such requests. Discuss as a possibility, not a plan.

Maintaining Candidacy (Semester GPA Requirement)

Once admitted, candidates must maintain a 3.0 semester GPA each semester. This is the GPA from that individual semester’s courses, not cumulative. At the department’s discretion, candidates who fall below 3.0 may continue one additional semester on probation. Candidates who do not meet the requirement will be dismissed.

A candidate may reapply once a 3.0 semester GPA has been obtained during a subsequent fall or spring semester.

Advising Students Who Are Conditionally Admitted or on Probation

The guidance below applies to both HPED and PETE students.

Step 1: Explain the Situation Clearly

  • Conditionally admitted: They have one semester to pass Praxis I or raise their past-two-years GPA to 3.0 (with that semester’s grades included).
  • Probationary: They must earn a 3.0 semester GPA this semester or they will be dismissed. Clarify this is not cumulative.

Step 2: Do the Math Together

Planning with the TU GPA Calculator: Use the Towson University GPA Calculator to run scenarios with the student. Enter only the current semester’s courses (not cumulative) to project what grades are needed for a 3.0 semester GPA.

Step 3: Build a Plan

  • Identify courses the student feels most and least confident in.
  • Discuss study strategies, tutoring, faculty office hours, Writing Center, or academic coaching.
  • If managing work or personal challenges, consider a Counseling Center referral.
  • For Praxis I path, help identify prep resources and a realistic test date.

Step 4: Set Check-Ins and Document

  • Encourage at least one mid-semester check-in.
  • Enter an SSC advising note with status, plan, resources, and follow-up dates.

Step 5: Discuss What Happens If They Fall Short

  • Conditional: Not fully admitted; may reapply at any point once requirements are met.
  • Probationary: Dismissed; may reapply after obtaining a 3.0 semester GPA in a subsequent semester.
  • Exemption: COE can approve up to 10%. Discuss as a possibility, not a plan.

9. Program Benefits & Talking Points

Use these points when advising prospective and current students:

  • Dual licensure = broader job eligibility and more employment opportunities
  • Strong job placement and stability in PK–12 settings
  • Job security is especially valuable in times of economic uncertainty
  • More class offerings and scheduling flexibility
  • Coursework efficiencies: only KNES 312 (not 311 + 313); only one A&P course if none taken yet
  • Quality of life: excellent benefits, summers off, family-friendly schedule
  • Transfer student advantage: nearly all graduates secure jobs; clear licensure pathway
  • Growth opportunities: master’s degree = higher salary potential
  • Additional career pathways: health, wellness, fitness, coaching, personal training
